When Do Power Tools Go on Sale? 2026 Sale Calendar

March 18, 2026 7 min read

Power tools follow a predictable sale cycle. If you understand the rhythm, you can save 20–50% on the exact same Milwaukee, DeWalt, or Makita kits that sell at full MSRP the rest of the year. The trick is knowing which sale events deliver real discounts versus the ones that just slap a "SALE" banner on regular prices.

This guide breaks down every major power tool sale event in 2026, month by month, with the specific discount ranges you can realistically expect and the brands that tend to discount most aggressively at each one.

The Big Three Sale Events (Don't Miss These)

Three sale events consistently deliver the deepest discounts on power tools every year. If you can only plan around a few dates, these are the ones:

  • Black Friday / Cyber Monday (November 27–30, 2026) — 30–50% off across all major brands. The single best time to buy.
  • Memorial Day (May 25, 2026) — 20–35% off. The biggest spring sale event for power tools.
  • Labor Day (September 7, 2026) — 20–35% off. End-of-summer clearance meets fall sale pricing.

Outside these three, you can still find solid deals, but the discounts typically top out at 15–25%. Let's walk through the full year.

January: New Year Sales and Holiday Clearance

Retailers need to move leftover holiday inventory fast. This creates a narrow window—usually the first two weeks of January—where you'll find 15–25% off on combo kits and individual bare tools that didn't sell during the Christmas rush.

Home Depot and Lowe's both run "New Year, New Projects" promotions. The discounts aren't as deep as Black Friday, but the selection of remaining Black Friday stock can include some gems. DeWalt 20V MAX combo kits (like the DCK240C2) often show up at $30–$50 below regular price. Ryobi ONE+ bundles—especially the starter kits with batteries included—tend to get marked down 20% or more as Home Depot clears shelf space.

Pro tip: Check clearance sections online and in-store separately. Home Depot's online clearance and in-store clearance are different inventories. You'll sometimes find better deals by visiting a physical store where a specific SKU has been sitting unsold.

February–March: Spring Black Friday at Home Depot

Home Depot's "Spring Black Friday" (sometimes branded "Spring Savings") typically runs in late March through mid-April. This has become one of the most reliable sale events for power tools outside of actual Black Friday. Discounts run 20–30% on select tools, with a heavy emphasis on Ryobi, Milwaukee, and DeWalt.

In past years, we've seen the Milwaukee M18 FUEL 1/2" Hammer Drill Kit (2804-22) drop from its $279 MSRP to around $199 during this event. DeWalt 20V MAX brushless kits regularly hit similar percentage discounts. Makita 18V LXT combo kits also see price cuts, though Makita tends to discount less aggressively than Milwaukee or DeWalt at this particular event.

February itself is relatively quiet for power tool sales. You might catch some Valentine's Day promotions, but they're almost always on general home improvement items, not serious power tools. Save your budget for Spring Black Friday.

April–May: Spring Savings and Memorial Day

April: Home Depot Spring Savings

Home Depot's Spring Savings event usually overlaps with the tail end of Spring Black Friday or runs as a separate event in early-to-mid April. Expect 15–25% off on outdoor power equipment and cordless tool kits. This is a strong time to buy Ryobi ONE+ 18V tools—Home Depot frequently runs "buy a kit, get a free tool" or "bonus battery" promotions on the Ryobi platform during spring.

May: Memorial Day (May 25, 2026)

Memorial Day is the biggest power tool sale between spring and fall. Both Home Depot and Lowe's go hard for 7–10 days leading up to the holiday weekend. Discounts typically range from 20–35%, with select doorbuster items hitting 40% off.

This is historically one of the best times to buy Milwaukee M18 and M12 platform tools. The M18 FUEL Circular Saw (2732-21HD) and the M18 FUEL SAWZALL (2821-21) frequently appear in Memorial Day ads at $30–$60 below MSRP. DeWalt runs aggressive combo kit deals—the 20V MAX 4-tool and 5-tool combo kits (like the DCK423D2 and DCK560D1M1) often reach their lowest non-Black Friday prices.

Pro tip: Memorial Day is also the best time to buy if you're starting a new cordless platform. Brands push "starter kit" bundles with extra batteries during this event to lock you into their ecosystem.

June: Father's Day Deals

Father's Day (June 21, 2026) drives a concentrated week of power tool promotions. The discounts aren't as deep as Memorial Day—typically 15–20% off—but brands release exclusive gift-oriented bundles that can represent solid value.

Expect to see Ryobi ONE+ gift kits (drill + impact driver + batteries + charger + bag) at $99–$129, DeWalt 20V MAX drill/driver kits under $100, and Milwaukee M12 compact tool kits positioned as gift items at 15–20% off. Amazon also runs Father's Day deals on power tools, sometimes with slightly different pricing than the big box stores.

If you missed Memorial Day, Father's Day gives you one more chance at decent spring pricing before the summer lull.

July: Amazon Prime Day and Independence Day

Independence Day (July 4)

Fourth of July sales are modest for power tools—10–15% off at Home Depot and Lowe's, mostly on outdoor and cordless tools. Not worth waiting for specifically, but not bad if you happen to need something that weekend.

Amazon Prime Day (Usually Mid-July)

Amazon Prime Day has become a legitimate power tool sale event. DeWalt, Makita, and Bosch tools tend to see the deepest Prime Day cuts on Amazon, with discounts of 20–35% on select items. Milwaukee tools are less commonly discounted on Amazon (Milwaukee controls distribution tightly), so don't hold your breath for M18 FUEL deals on Prime Day.

The catch: Prime Day pricing is volatile. Deals appear and expire within hours. Price tracking tools are essential for catching the best Prime Day discounts before they vanish. Some "deals" are also inflated-then-discounted, so knowing a tool's actual price history helps you spot real bargains versus manufactured urgency.

Home Depot and Lowe's both run competing "anti-Prime Day" sales. These can actually be better for Milwaukee and Ryobi tools since those brands have stronger distribution through Home Depot than Amazon.

August: The Quiet Month

August is the deadest month for power tool sales. Retailers are between summer and fall cycles, and there are no major holidays to anchor promotions. If you see a deal in August, it's likely a clearance price on a discontinued model or a retailer-specific promotion—not a seasonal sale.

Use August to research what you want to buy and set up price alerts so you're ready for Labor Day.

September: Labor Day

Labor Day (September 7, 2026) is the fall equivalent of Memorial Day for power tool deals. Both Home Depot and Lowe's run 7–10 day events with 20–35% off across major brands. Amazon joins in with its own competing sale.

Labor Day is particularly strong for combo kits. If you want to buy a DeWalt 20V MAX or FLEXVOLT system with multiple tools, batteries, and a charger, this is your window. We've tracked DeWalt FLEXVOLT combo kits dropping $80–$120 during Labor Day sales in previous years. Makita 18V LXT 5-tool combo kits (like the XT507PG) also hit annual lows around Labor Day.

For Milwaukee buyers, Labor Day is the second-best time of year (behind Black Friday) to buy M18 FUEL tools. The M18 FUEL Drill/Impact combo (2997-22) typically drops from $349 to around $259–$279.

October: Early Black Friday Previews

October used to be quiet, but "Black Friday creep" has turned it into a preview month. Home Depot often publishes its first holiday tool deals in late October, and Amazon runs early Black Friday sales as well.

The October deals are real—15–25% off on select tools—but they're typically not as deep as the actual Black Friday doorbusters. If you see something you need at 20% off in October, it's reasonable to buy. But if you can wait three more weeks, Black Friday will likely beat that price by another 10–15%.

November: Black Friday and Cyber Monday

This is it. The single best month of the year to buy power tools, full stop. Black Friday (November 27, 2026) and the surrounding two weeks deliver the deepest discounts you'll find on every major brand.

What to Expect

  • Milwaukee: M18 FUEL kits 30–40% off. The M18 FUEL 2-tool combo (2997-22) has historically hit $199 on Black Friday, down from $349 MSRP. Individual bare tools drop 25–35%.
  • DeWalt: 20V MAX and FLEXVOLT combo kits 30–50% off. DeWalt is typically the most aggressive discounter on Black Friday. Expect 5-tool combo kits under $300 and drill/driver kits under $100.
  • Makita: 18V LXT and 40V XGT tools 25–35% off. Makita doesn't discount as deeply as DeWalt, but 40V XGT tools—still relatively new to the market—see their best prices of the year on Black Friday.
  • Ryobi: ONE+ 18V kits 30–50% off. Ryobi already sits at a lower price point, and Black Friday pricing makes it the cheapest way to build out a cordless platform. Expect complete drill/impact kits under $80.
  • Hilti: Hilti rarely discounts through Home Depot or Amazon, but they occasionally run direct promotions on hilti.com. Expect free battery promotions rather than price cuts.
Pro tip: Home Depot and Lowe's both release their Black Friday tool deals online before in-store. Most deals go live the Sunday or Monday before Thanksgiving. You don't need to stand in line.

Cyber Monday

Cyber Monday (November 30, 2026) pricing is usually identical to Black Friday at Home Depot and Lowe's. Amazon sometimes has unique Cyber Monday deals on DeWalt and Makita tools that weren't part of their Black Friday lineup. Don't assume Cyber Monday is automatically better or worse—it's essentially a second wave.

December: Holiday Sales and Christmas Clearance

Early December keeps some Black Friday pricing alive—many retailers extend "Holiday Savings" events through mid-December. Discounts of 20–30% are common on the same tools that were 30–50% off on Black Friday.

The real December opportunity comes after Christmas. December 26 through January 1 is clearance season. Retailers markdown remaining holiday inventory aggressively, and you can occasionally beat Black Friday pricing on specific SKUs that didn't sell through. This is especially true for budget-friendly cordless drill kits and combo sets that were overstocked.

Price-Matching: Your Secret Weapon

Both Home Depot and Lowe's offer price-matching policies that can save you money during any sale event:

  • Home Depot price-matches select online retailers (Amazon, Lowe's, and others) on identical, in-stock items. You can request a price match in-store or through customer service online.
  • Lowe's offers a similar policy, matching prices from local competitors and major online retailers on identical items.

This means if Home Depot has a lower price than Lowe's (or vice versa), you can often get the lower price at whichever store you prefer. During sale events, prices between the two retailers can differ by $10–$30 on the same tool, so it pays to check both before buying.

Pro tip: Home Depot's price-match policy includes Amazon pricing. If a DeWalt or Makita tool drops lower on Amazon during Prime Day, you can sometimes price-match it at Home Depot and pick it up in-store the same day instead of waiting for shipping.

The Bottom Line: Time Your Purchase Right

If you need a power tool today, buy it today—waiting six months to save $40 doesn't make sense if the project is stalled. But if you have flexibility, the power tools sale schedule is remarkably consistent year to year. Black Friday remains the undisputed champion for deepest discounts. Memorial Day and Labor Day are your best bets outside of November. And Home Depot's Spring Black Friday has quietly become one of the most valuable sale events of the year.

The real challenge isn't knowing when sales happen. It's knowing whether a specific "deal" is actually a good price or just marketing noise. That's where price history tracking becomes invaluable—a tool that was "30% off" but never actually sold at the inflated original price isn't a deal at all.
